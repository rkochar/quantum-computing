In this problem you will have to implement the quantum Fourier transform for nn qubits.

  • input: nn number of qubits
  • output: a quantum circuit impementing FNFN with N=2nN=2n:

FN=1N−−√⎛⎝⎜⎜⎜ω0⋅0N⋮ω(N−1)⋅0N……ω0⋅(N−1)N⋮ω(N−1)⋅(N−1)N⎞⎠⎟⎟⎟FN=1N(ωN0⋅0…ωN0⋅(N−1)⋮⋮ωN(N−1)⋅0…ωN(N−1)⋅(N−1))

where
ωN=ei2πNωN=ei2πN

is the NN-th root of unity.

This is a gate that performs the transformation:

FN|xn−1⟩…|x0⟩=1N−−√∑yn−1,…,y0∈{0,1}ωxyN|yn−1⟩…|y0⟩FN|xn−1⟩…|x0⟩=1N∑yn−1,…,y0∈{0,1}ωNxy|yn−1⟩…|y0⟩

where x,yx,y are two integers such that
x=∑i=0n−12ixi,y=∑i=0n−12iyix=∑i=0n−12ixi,y=∑i=0n−12iyi

This transformation is implemented by a circuit acting on an n qubit register. Position 0 in the register corresponds with the least-significant-bit.

The only allowed gate is one qiskit.extensions.unitary.UnitaryGate, i.e. the QFT needs to be constructed by definition. Any additional gates, such as barriers, must not be included.
